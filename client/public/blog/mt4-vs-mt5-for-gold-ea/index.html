MT4 vs MT5 for a Gold EA: Which Should You Run?

By the XAUUSD Robot Team · Updated July 2026

Quick answer: For most gold traders today, MT5 is the better platform for a XAUUSD EA — it has a faster strategy tester, a built-in economic calendar for news filters, and more timeframes and order types. But MT4 is still perfectly capable, lighter, and widely supported. The real deciding factor is simple: run whichever platform your broker and your EA both support. The core difference

MetaTrader 4 (2005) and MetaTrader 5 (2010) are both made by the same company, MetaQuotes. MT5 was meant to replace MT4 — but MT4 stayed so popular with forex traders that both are still everywhere today.

The most important thing to understand: they are not compatible with each other. An EA written for MT4 uses the MQL4 language; an MT5 EA uses MQL5. A developer has to build and maintain a separate version for each platform. So "which platform" isn't just a preference — it decides which build of the EA you install.

What actually matters for a gold EA

Forget the feature-list for a second — here's what genuinely affects an automated XAU/USD strategy:

  • Backtesting accuracy. Gold is volatile, so testing on real tick data matters. MT5's multi-threaded tester with real ticks is meaningfully better and faster than MT4's. If you plan to backtest your gold EA properly, MT5 is the stronger tool.
  • News handling. Gold reacts violently to NFP, FOMC and CPI. MT5's built-in economic calendar lets a news filter know when to stand aside — on MT4 that data has to come from an external source.
  • Execution. Both are fast when paired with a good ECN broker and a VPS. MQL5's more modern engine has a slight edge, but broker and VPS matter far more than the platform here.
  • Broker support. The practical constraint: some brokers offer only one platform, or better gold spreads on one. That often makes the decision for you.

Reality check: the platform does not change the strategy's edge. MT5 helps a good EA run and be tested the way it was designed — it will not rescue a weak strategy. Choose the platform, then judge the EA on its own merits.

When MT5 wins

Pick MT5 if any of these apply:

  • You want to backtest seriously — the tester alone is worth it.
  • Your strategy uses a news filter and you want the built-in calendar.
  • You trade gold plus other assets (crypto, indices) from one platform.
  • Your broker offers tighter gold spreads or better execution on MT5.
  • You're starting fresh with no reason to be tied to MT4.

When MT4 is fine

Stay on MT4 if:

  • Your EA is MT4-only and proven — no need to change what works.
  • Your preferred broker only offers MT4 for gold.
  • You want the lightest possible footprint on a small VPS.
  • You're already comfortable and everything runs smoothly.

MT4 isn't "outdated and broken" — millions of gold traders still use it daily. It's simply the older, lighter option with fewer built-in extras.

Should you switch?

If you're already running a gold EA happily on MT4, there's no urgent reason to move. But if you're setting up from scratch and your broker supports both, start on MT5 — you get the better tester, the calendar and more room to grow, at no real downside. And because a well-built gold EA ships both versions, you can switch later without changing robots.

Frequently asked questions

Is MT4 or MT5 better for a gold EA?

MT5 for most traders today — faster tester, built-in calendar, more timeframes and order types. MT4 is still capable and lighter, and fine if that's what your broker and EA support.

Can the same EA run on both?

Not the same file — MT4 uses MQL4, MT5 uses MQL5, so a developer provides a separate build for each. Many gold EAs, including ours, ship both.

Does MT5 make an EA more profitable?

No. It improves execution, testing and news handling, but can't turn a losing strategy into a winner.

Is MT5 harder to use?

The workflow is nearly identical — install, attach to a XAUUSD chart, enable auto-trading. MT5 just has more features.

Should I switch from MT4?

If your broker and EA support MT5, it's worth it for the tester and calendar. If everything MT4 works, no urgent need.
