What a forex VPS is

A VPS (Virtual Private Server) is a small, always-on computer in a data centre that runs your MetaTrader platform and gold EA around the clock. Instead of relying on your home PC, the robot lives on a professional server with backup power and a fast connection.

For automated gold trading, that reliability is the whole point. A dropped connection at the wrong moment can leave a trade unmanaged; a VPS keeps the EA running without interruption.

Do you need one for a gold EA?

If you run your EA only for a few hours while watching the screen, you can manage without one. But most automated traders want the robot active 24/5 — and that is where a VPS becomes almost essential.

  • No downtime from power cuts, restarts or internet drops.
  • Lower latency to the broker, which reduces slippage.
  • Freedom — your own machine can be off while the EA trades.

Reality check: a VPS will not turn a losing EA into a winner. It removes avoidable execution problems so a good strategy can perform as intended — nothing more.

What actually matters in a gold EA VPS

  • Latency to your broker. Under 10 ms is excellent; under 1 ms is ideal for scalping. Place the server near your broker's data centre.
  • Server location. Match hubs like London, New York, Amsterdam, Chicago, Singapore or Tokyo to where your broker's server lives.
  • Uptime. Look for a guaranteed 99.9% or higher, backed by redundant power and network.
  • Hardware. SSD or NVMe storage, modern CPU, and enough RAM for your platforms.
  • Windows Server. MT4 and MT5 are Windows-native, so a Windows VPS is the safe choice.
  • Support and RDP access so you can monitor and fix issues quickly.

Expert insight: find your broker's server location inside MT4/MT5, then choose a VPS in that city. Matching the two is the single biggest factor in getting low, stable latency.

How to set it up

  1. Pick a VPS near your broker's server with the specs above.
  2. Connect via RDP (Remote Desktop) to the Windows server.
  3. Install MetaTrader and log into your account.
  4. Copy your gold EA into the Experts folder and attach it to the XAUUSD chart.
  5. Enable automated trading and confirm the EA is active.
  6. Check latency and uptime, then leave it running 24/5.

Common mistakes

  • Choosing a VPS far from the broker — high latency defeats the purpose.
  • Under-spec'ing RAM, especially for MT5 or multiple terminals.
  • Assuming a VPS improves the strategy — it only improves reliability.
  • Ignoring uptime guarantees and picking on price alone.
  • Never checking latency after setup.

Best practices

  1. Match VPS location to broker server for the lowest latency.
  2. Start small and scale specs as you add EAs.
  3. Use Windows Server for full MetaTrader compatibility.
  4. Confirm 99.9%+ uptime before committing.
  5. Monitor latency and the EA's status regularly.

Frequently asked questions

If you want 24/5 trading without your PC on, yes. It keeps the robot running through outages and can cut execution delay.

Under 10 ms is excellent; under 1 ms is ideal for scalping, achieved by placing the VPS near your broker.

About 2 GB RAM and 1 vCPU per terminal; more for several terminals or EA-heavy setups, with SSD/NVMe and Windows Server.

How much does it cost?

Basic plans start at a few dollars a month; a single gold EA rarely needs an expensive one.

Does a VPS make my EA profitable?

No. It improves uptime and execution but cannot fix a weak strategy.
